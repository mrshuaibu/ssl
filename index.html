What is SSL

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a technology that encrypts the data exchanged between a user's browser and a website, ensuring that sensitive information like passwords and credit card details remain private. It’s what keeps communication secure when you visit websites. You might have noticed a small padlock icon in the browser’s address bar—this indicates that SSL is active.

What is TLS?

TLS (Transport Layer Security) is the updated and more secure version of SSL. Though we often refer to “SSL” in general terms, most modern websites use TLS. Both SSL and TLS aim to protect data, but TLS offers better security and performance.

How can I tell that my webpage is secure?

A webpage is secure if it shows the padlock icon in the browser’s address bar. You’ll also notice that the web address starts with "https" (instead of "http"). This means the website is using SSL or TLS to encrypt the data you send and receive.

What does it mean to be secure?

When a webpage is secure, it means that the information you share (like login credentials or personal data) is encrypted, preventing hackers from easily intercepting or tampering with it.

What is encryption and why do I care?

Encryption is the process of scrambling data so that only authorized parties can understand it. It’s like sending a coded message that can only be decoded by the right person. Without encryption, your private information could be intercepted and misused by attackers. So, encryption ensures privacy and security.

Do the prefixes HTTP or HTTPS matter when we're talking about secure websites?

Yes, they matter! "HTTP" stands for HyperText Transfer Protocol and is used for transmitting data over the web, but it’s not secure. "HTTPS" adds an "S," which stands for "Secure." The "S" indicates that the website is using SSL/TLS encryption to protect the data being transferred.

What is an SSL certificate authority?

An SSL certificate authority (CA) is a trusted organization that issues SSL certificates to websites. These certificates verify that the website is legitimate and trustworthy, ensuring that users are connecting to the correct and secure server.
